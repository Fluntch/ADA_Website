Now, partner, it’s time to score these trusty steeds and determine which ones guide our cowboys through the rugged trails. Here's how we measure the “goodness” of articles:

Prioritizing Scores Based on Minimal Clicks

1. Path Score: Measures how closely the cowboy's path matches the shortest route. A score of 1 means the most efficient route.

Formula: Path Score = Optimal Distance / Simplified Path Length

2. Article Scoring Based on Path Weights

There are two ways to calculate article scores based on path weights:

  • Weighted Average: Average path weights for each article.
  • Sum of Centered Weights: Compute the mean article weight and sum the centered weights for each article.

Scores Based on Detours

3. Detour Ratio: Compares the number of detours an article causes to its total appearances. Lower ratios are better.

Formula: Detour Ratio = Detour Count / Total Appearances

Scores Based on Unfinished Paths

4. Unfinished Ratio: Measures how often an article appears in incomplete paths. Higher ratios suggest failure.

Formula: Unfinished Ratio = Unfinished Count / Total Appearances

Scores for Faster Completion

5. Weighted Average of Article Speed: Calculates the average speed for articles based on the time per path length. Faster paths score higher.

Formula: Article Speed = Sum of Path Speeds / Number of Paths

6. Sum of Centered Article Speed: Measures how articles contribute to faster paths, centered by mean path speed.

Formula: Centered Speed = Path Speed - Mean Path Speed

Note: Scaling is applied so that higher values always indicate better performance, even if lower ratios are preferred in certain metrics.
