Finished Trails

When a cowboy successfully reaches their target, it’s a trail well-blazed! But even among these, we have to ensure fairness and accuracy. Here's how we sift out only the best trails :

  • One Trail Per Cowboy-Target Pair
    Cowboys are smart—they sometimes retrace their steps to learn the best route. However, we only keep the first unique trail for each cowboy and trail pair to ensure we aren’t biased by repeated attempts.
  • Fair Share of Trails
    Some trails are overly popular—like the shortcut everyone loves. We cap the number of times a trail can appear (no more than 5 for each start-target pair), ensuring no trail overshadows others.
  • No Trails to Nowhere
    A horse can’t travel to the same spot it starts from—those trails (with distance = 0) are removed.
  • Outliers Beware
    Using the Interquartile Range (IQR) method, we rope in the good trails and leave out the statistical outlaws. For every group of trails with the same minimum distance (number of clicks to target), we remove those with extreme lengths or durations.
